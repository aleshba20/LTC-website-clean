    What is covered in our Photography courses?

    Our Photography track teaches complete workflow: camera exposure (aperture, shutter speed, ISO), lenses and focal lengths, lighting (natural & artificial), composition rules, shooting genres, and professional editing in Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop. Learners develop portfolio-ready images through guided shoots and critiques.

    You‚Äôll practice planning a shoot, selecting gear, capturing RAW, culling efficiently, color-correcting, retouching, exporting for web and print, and presenting a cohesive set. Sessions include studio and on-location exercises tailored for portrait, product, event, and street photography.

    What‚Äôs Covered in the Photography Course?

    The syllabus emphasizes hands-on practice with instructor feedback.

    • Camera Basics: exposure triangle, metering, focus modes, white balance, RAW vs JPEG.
    • Lenses & Gear: primes vs zooms, focal length & compression, tripods, filters.
    • Lighting: golden hour, reflectors, speedlights, softboxes, continuous vs strobe, basic off-camera flash.
    • Composition: rule of thirds, leading lines, framing, color harmony, visual balance.
    • Genres: portrait, product, street, landscape, event, food & real estate.
    • Post-Production: Lightroom catalog, culling, presets, local adjustments, Photoshop retouching, export for web/print.

    Prerequisites

    • Any interchangeable-lens camera or advanced compact is fine.
    • Basic computer skills; Lightroom/Photoshop installed for editing units.
    • Comfort shooting in Aperture/Shutter Priority; we‚Äôll move to Manual.

    Photography Learning Path

    Progress through structured modules from exposure and composition to controlled lighting and professional retouching. Each stage builds toward a publishable portfolio and client-ready delivery.

    Beginner (Essentials)

    • Manual exposure: aperture, shutter, ISO; histograms & metering
    • Autofocus modes, back-button focus, hand-holding techniques
    • Natural light basics, reflectors, on-camera flash etiquette
    • Composition drills and quick Lightroom introduction

    Intermediate (Lighting & Genres)

    • Speedlights & modifiers, bounce vs direct, color gels
    • Portrait, product, food, and event workflows
    • Location scouting, permits, client brief interpretation
    • Lightroom develop workflow & color management basics

    Advanced (Studio & Retouching)

    • Off-camera lighting, two- and three-light setups, rim & hair light
    • Tethered shooting, flags, scrims, and backgrounds
    • Photoshop skin retouching, frequency separation, dodge & burn
    • Export pipelines for print, social, and client delivery

    Assessment & Projects

    • Genre mini-projects (portrait/product/event/real-estate)
    • Lighting challenge: recreate a reference image
    • Portfolio review & critique with sequencing and captions
    • Client-style brief: shoot, edit, export, and deliver package

    Entry Requirements

    • Own or have access to a camera (DSLR/Mirrorless recommended)
    • Laptop with Lightroom Classic & Photoshop for post-production
    • Willingness to shoot weekly assignments

Photo Editing ‚Äî Adobe Photoshop, Lightroom & Retouching Workflow

What is covered in our Photo Editing courses?

The program teaches the complete post-processing pipeline: importing RAW files, organizing assets, non-destructive editing with layers and masks, color correction and grading, retouching (skin, product, real-estate), and mastering/export for web and print. You‚Äôll build repeatable workflows using presets, actions, and smart objects.

Tools covered include Adobe Photoshop for advanced retouching and compositing, Adobe Lightroom Classic for cataloging and global edits, and Camera Raw for RAW development. Sessions are hands-on with real images, focusing on quality, speed, and output standards.





Career Outcomes & Recognition

Skills developed in this program map to roles such as:

  • Photo Editor / Retoucher (portrait, product, real-estate)
  • Content Designer / Social Media Graphics
  • E-commerce Image Specialist
  • Creative Assistant for studios and agencies

Graduates build a portfolio with before/after edits across different genres, with deliverables aligned to platform and print specifications (web JPEG, PNG transparency, CMYK print, and high-resolution TIFF).

What‚Äôs Covered in the Photo Editing Course?

The syllabus emphasizes practical editing with instructor feedback.

  • Foundations: RAW vs JPEG, color spaces (sRGB/AdobeRGB), bit depth, resolution, non-destructive editing principles.
  • Lightroom / ACR: exposure, white balance, curves, HSL, lens corrections, noise reduction, detail sharpening, crop & transform.
  • Photoshop Core: layers, masks, selections (Select Subject, channels), adjustment layers, smart objects, blend modes.
  • Retouching: skin cleanup, frequency separation, dodge & burn, liquify, blemish removal, texture preservation, product dust cleanup.
  • Color & Look: curves, selective color, gradient maps, LUTs, matching sets, creative toning for brand consistency.
  • Delivery: export presets, sharpening for web/print, metadata/watermarking, file formats, batch processing, action automation.

Prerequisites

  • Laptop capable of running Photoshop (SSD recommended, 16 GB RAM preferred).
  • Adobe Photoshop installed; Lightroom Classic recommended (trial acceptable).
  • Basic computer literacy; no prior retouching experience required.

Photo Editing Learning Path

Progress from RAW development and essential layer/mask techniques to professional retouching, color grading, and compositing. Each stage builds toward a client-ready portfolio and efficient delivery workflow.

Beginner (Essentials)

  • Lightroom/ACR basics: exposure, color, lens & transform tools
  • Photoshop interface, layers, masks, adjustment layers
  • Selections: Quick Selection, Select Subject, refine hair/edges
  • Global color/contrast looks; export for web

Intermediate (Retouching & Color)

  • Skin cleanup with non-destructive tools (Heal/Clone, Dodge & Burn)
  • Frequency separation with action safety and texture protection
  • Product/real-estate cleanup, perspective fixes, sky replacement
  • Color grading with curves, selective color, gradient maps, LUTs

Advanced (Frequency Separation & Compositing)

  • Advanced masking (channels, luminance ranges) and blend-if
  • Smart objects, linked files, reusable mockups and templates
  • Compositing techniques: shadows, perspective match, color match
  • Print workflows: soft proofing, CMYK prep, sharpening strategies

Assessment & Projects

  • Portrait retouch: natural skin finish, eyes, hair, color style
  • Product cleanup: dust removal, reflections, background isolation
  • Real-estate/architecture set: lens corrections, sky & window balance
  • Final portfolio: export for web (sRGB) and print (300 DPI, CMYK)

Entry Requirements

  • Laptop meeting Photoshop minimum specs; SSD storage recommended
  • Photoshop installed; Lightroom Classic recommended
  • Practice images provided; using your own RAWs encouraged

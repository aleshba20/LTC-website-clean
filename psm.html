Process Safety Management (PSM)

Preventing major accidents in high-hazard industries

What is Process Safety Management?

Process Safety Management (PSM) is a systematic approach to identify, evaluate, and control risks associated with highly hazardous chemical processesâ€”reducing the potential for fires, explosions, and toxic releases. The course is built around international standards including OSHA 29 CFR 1910.119 and CCPS guidelines.

Typical sectors: oil & gas refineries, petrochemicals, manufacturing, power & utilities, and chemical storage/distribution facilities.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the 14 key elements of OSHAâ€™s PSM standard.
  • Perform risk identification, Process Hazard Analysis (PHA), and control selection.
  • Establish, implement, and audit effective safety management systems.
  • Strengthen capabilities in emergency response, Management of Change (MOC), and compliance auditing.
  • Promote proactive safety leadership and culture.

Key Topics Covered

  • Regulatory foundations: OSHA 29 CFR 1910.119, CCPS guidance, and compliance needs.
  • Process Hazard Analysis (PHA): HAZOP, FMEA, and What-If analysis.
  • Operating procedures & safe work: permits to work, isolation/LOTO, and SOPs.
  • Mechanical integrity: inspection, testing, and preventive maintenance for critical assets.
  • Management of Change (MOC): risk reviews during modifications, documentation control.
  • Incident investigation: root-cause analysis, corrective and preventive actions.
  • Emergency planning & response: drills, coordination, and readiness.
  • Compliance audits & improvement: performance monitoring and continual improvement.

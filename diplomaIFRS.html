Diploma In IFRS By ACCA

International Financial Reporting Standards IFRS

Diploma in International Financial Reporting (DipIFR) is an international qualification in IFRS developed by the leading professional accounting organisation ‚Äì Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA) ‚Äì which helps to develop knowledge in the area of International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). The diploma is widely accepted in more than 80 countries. As with other countries around the world that have converged to IFRS, it has become increasingly relevant worldwide. International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) are mandated in more than 100 countries worldwide.

Course Objectives

The course is designed to develop your knowledge and understanding of International Financial Reporting Standards. You will also learn how to apply them, as well as the concepts and principles which underpin them.

On completion of the Diploma, candidates should be able to:

  • Understand, explain and apply the IASB‚Äôs Conceptual Framework for Financial Reporting.
  • Apply relevant financial reporting standards to key elements of financial reports.
  • Identify and apply disclosure requirements for companies in financial reports and notes.
  • Prepare group financial statements (excluding group cash-flow statements) including subsidiaries, associates and joint arrangements.

Who is it For?

If you are a professional accountant or auditor who works in practice or business, and are qualified according to national accounting standards, you are eligible to take this ACCA financial reporting qualification. If you are working in practice but not yet qualified, you may still be eligible.

You will need to prove that you meet one of the following:

  • Two years‚Äô relevant accounting experience and a relevant degree (attracting at least ACCA qualification exemptions for the Applied Knowledge and Corporate & Business Law (LW) exams).
  • Two years‚Äô relevant accounting experience and an ACCA Certificate in International Financial Reporting.
  • Three years‚Äô relevant accounting experience.

Program Syllabus

A. International Sources of Authority

  • The International Accounting Standards Board (IASB) and the regulatory framework.

B. Elements of Financial Statements

  • Revenue recognition.
  • Property, plant and equipment.
  • Impairment of assets.
  • Leases.
  • Intangible assets and goodwill.
  • Inventories.
  • Financial instruments.
  • Liabilities ‚Äì provisions, contingent assets and liabilities.

C. Presentation and Additional Disclosures

  • The IASB and the regulatory framework (presentation requirements).
  • Presentation of the statement of financial position and the statement of profit or loss and other comprehensive income.
  • Earnings per share.
  • Events after the reporting date.
  • Accounting policies, changes in estimates and errors; related party disclosures.
  • Operating segments.
  • Reporting requirements of small and medium-sized entities (SMEs).

D. Preparation of External Financial Reports for Combined Entities

  • Preparation of group consolidated external reports.
  • Business combinations ‚Äì intra-group adjustments.
  • Business combinations ‚Äì fair value adjustments.
  • Business combinations ‚Äì associates and joint arrangements.
